Hypothesis Testing on Zillow housing data set


 The hypothesis asked to demonstrate that the price of houses in university towns is less affected by the recession from 2009.

 To do this, I ran a T-test to compare the ratio of the mean price of houses in university towns the quarter before the recession starts (2008 quarter 2) compared to the recession bottom (2009 quarter 2). The hypothesis proved to be correct.

  •Used: Jupyter Notebook, Python, Pandas, Numpy, Scipy

Correlation among cryptocurrencies, fiat, and gold

 End to end project which aimed to show if any correlation exists or not among cryptocurrencies, cryptocurrencies and fiat, and, between cryptocurrencies and gold.

 Steps:

  - State the research question: “What is the relation between cryptocurrencies BTC, LTC, ETH, Stock Market, and Gold in the past 10 years?”

  - Provide trusted sources for the datasets (BTC, LTC, ETH, Gold, and Stock Market currency in US Dollar) at a 1-day granularity. I used finance.yahoo.com and quandl.com.

  - Processed the data into data frames and decided to do 4 subplots, 3 simple line plots, and a matrix plot.

 Having to deal with time-series data, line charts were the typical form of representation.

 It was also easy to spot when prices dropped or increased, and to make a fast relative comparison between currencies.

 The heated histogram was picturing a negative correlation between the US Dollar and Gold and a positive linear relationship among cryptocurrencies (BTC, ETH, LTC).

  •Used: Jupyter Notebook, Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn

Electoral turnout map for the Romanian elections - "parlament2016.ro"

 The main purpose of the site was to show the electoral turnout live (less than 2 minutes delay from the official government data) for the Romanian parliamentary elections which took place on the 11th of December 2016.
